ACT Rules Overview

W3C Accessibility Conformance Testing (ACT) Rules are publicy documented ways for testing the conformance of content to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG). ACT Rules are contributed, developed, and vetted by the community, to support transparency and consensus. They are intended to be used in the development of fully- and semi-automated testing tools, and manual evaluation methodologies for web accessibility.

List of ACT Rules

List of ACT Rules is currently only for demonstration purposes, to show how such ACT Rules could look like.

ACT Review Process

ACT Review Process defines the community process for contributing, developing, and reviewing ACT Rules.

ACT Rules Development

ACT Rules can be developed by different communities. Currently the Auto-WCAG Community Group is an active place of rules development.

ACT Rules Format

The ACT Rules Format specification defines the requirements for writing accessibility test rules, and is the basis for ACT.
